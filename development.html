Development

Context/Problem:

The problem I set out to solve was the frustrating experience users often face when shopping for t-shirts and tees online. Many existing websites lack intuitive filtering and sorting options, leading to a cluttered and inefficient shopping journey. Recognizing the need for a solution that prioritizes simplicity and functionality, I aimed to create a platform that would streamline the online shopping experience for users.

Research:

My research involved conducting a thorough competitive analysis of three prominent websites: Zara, Redbubble, and Not On The High Street. Through this research, I gained valuable insights into the filtering, sorting, and aggregation features that resonated with users. My target audience comprised individuals seeking quality t-shirts and tees online, who valued ease of navigation and personalized shopping experiences.

Process:

My design process was iterative, guided by insights from my research and user-centric principles. I explored various filtering and sorting options, considering the tradeoffs between complexity and usability. Iterating on my designs, I strategically prioritized essential features such as size, color, and price filtering, while minimizing visual clutter and maintaining a minimalist aesthetic. Despite being constrained by assignment guidelines, I remained adaptable, ensuring my design met both user needs and project requirements.

Solution:

My solution, Sandy's T-Shirts and Tees, showcases a refined and user-friendly marketplace for online shopping. By implementing intuitive filtering and sorting mechanisms inspired by my research, I created an environment where users can effortlessly find their perfect t-shirt or tee. The inclusion of a dynamic cart feature ensures a seamless transition from browsing to purchasing, enhancing the overall user experience.

Learnings:

Throughout my design process, I gained valuable insights into the importance of prioritizing user needs and maintaining simplicity in design. I learned to balance functionality with visual aesthetics, making informed decisions to optimize usability. My experience highlighted the significance of iterative design and the value of adapting to constraints while remaining focused on delivering a superior user experience.

Role:

I played the role of both creator and curator, spearheading the development of Sandy's T-Shirts and Tees. While not part of a team, I collaborated with TA's for guidance and feedback, leveraging my research and design expertise to craft a solution tailored to the needs of my audience. Through my dedication and strategic decision-making, I demonstrated my commitment to delivering a user-centric marketplace for t-shirts and tees.
