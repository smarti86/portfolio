Problem:

CUR8, a carbon removal brokerage with a bold mission to eradicate 1 billion tonnes of carbon annually, faced a challenge: its lack of a purchasing process was slowing their noble cause. The existing over-the-phone portfolio creation method slowed down customer acquisition, leading to a pressing need for a streamlined solution.

After discussing with my team--Angie Osei-Ampadu, Joyce Li, and Ashley Cai--the mission was clear: simplify the process and empower users to purchase carbon removal portfolios directly from the CUR8 website.

Research:

Starting our journey, we delved into a competitive analysis, seeking inspiration from industry leaders like Lune and Frontier. By dissecting their approaches and referencing CUR8's objectives, we identified their current competitive advantage in the purchase flow process:

  • Lune's sectioned journey to curate carbon credit portfolios
  • Frontier's portfolio breakdown and projects details

Our peers and TAs also gave us feedback on our low-fidelity mockup. With their help, we added a feature for stakeholder collaboration for an intuitive, frictionless purchasing flow. With these insights, we embarked on a journey of innovation and design.

Process Details:

Our design iterations began with independent sketches, resulting in eight distinct flows. These are two examples, the first being my own:

For our first iteration wireframing:
We consolidated our ideas, focusing on 4 essential pages: portfolio summary, approval, cart, and confirmation.

For our second iteration wireframing:
Feedback from CUR8 and peers guided our design decisions, leading us to make adjustments, like adding back buttons for usability and adding a feature for stakeholder collaboration.

For our last iteration wireframing:
Focusing solely on payment pages that I developed, users review their portfolio and proceed to the purchase request page. If stakeholders weren't previously invited, a banner alert prompts users to do so. Upon submission, users receive a confirmation message to return to their portfolio or exit.

From these diverse blueprints, we crafted wireframes, refining our ideas into tangible prototypes. Iteration by iteration, we honed our designs, focusing on pivotal pages like portfolio summaries, carts, and confirmations. Each iteration brought us closer to our vision of a frictionless journey for CUR8's customers.

Solution:

Our final design comprised a cohesive purchasing flow tailored to CUR8's requirements.

The flow seamlessly guided users through project selection, stakeholder collaboration, and purchase request submission. Features like project breakdowns, stakeholder feedback, and a receipt-like review page enhanced user understanding and engagement. By aligning with CUR8's brand identity and providing a user-friendly experience, our solution aimed to streamline the carbon removal portfolio purchasing process.
