Problem:

In the realm of high-stakes mergers and acquisitions, where every dollar counts, my Corporate Finance course group was tasked with finding a fair price for LVMH's potential acquisition of Tiffany & Co.
The recommendated price is based on a variety of comprehensive valuations, including Comparable Companies, Precedent Transactions, Discounted Cash Flow (DCF), and Leveraged Buyout (LBO) analysis methodologies.

Research:

To begin the various analysis methodologies, we needed to produce Assumptions on Tiffany & Co.'s growth for the Base Case and Synergy Case. The Assumptions included projecting revenue growth projected for each main region for the next 3 years. Below is the Base Case Financials and Assumptions example:

For the Synergy Case, these are the Assumptions:

The audience includes instructors and team members, seeking a thorough understanding of the valuation process and recommendations.

Process Details:

After we agreed on the assumptions, we had the tools to begin our independent evaluations. As I was responsible for the LBO, I first established the Sources & Uses of funds, then I created a Debt Schedule for the financing required for the transaction. Finally, I found a purchase prices that would fit within the desired 20-25% range for the Internal Rate of Return (IRR). Those prices were $71.99 and $67.49, respectively.

Internal Rate of Return - 20%

Internal Rate of Return - 25%

My biggest challenge was often finding the correct line items to include in my evaluation. Given that this was the first LBO I completed, I was lost on what to cherry pick from the vast information available. To address the challenge and continue to learn, I used Wall Street Prep, a site with financial modeling self-study courses.

Solution:

Armed with a comprehensive valuation analysis, we crafted our final report and recommendation: LVMH should initiate negotiations with Tiffany & Co., proposing a purchase price of $133.00 per share—a figure that encapsulated both the company's intrinsic value and the strategic synergies that will be created.

View Report!

Learnings:

  • The importance of thorough financial analysis to understand and defend the suggested purchase price of Tiffany & Co.
  • Garbage In, Garbage Out. Assumptions have a drastic impact on valuation outcomes and need to be crafted carefully.
  • Team collaboration, attention to detail, and effective communication was crucial to producing the best recommendation and learning the most from the experience.

Role:

As the Leveraged Buyout (LBO) Lead Analyst, my analysis focused on finding the purchase price per share of Tiffany & Co. that will allow LVMH to maximize their investment while still being able to repay the debt taken on by the transaction.

My teammates George Bader, Willian Silva, Ekaterina Vanurina, and Justin Weiss completed the WACC Analysis, Comparable Companies, Precedent Transactions, Discounted Cash Flow (DCF). Collaborating together, we reviewed all of the valuation methods, explained our decisions, and taught one another what we learned throughout the process.
