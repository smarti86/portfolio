Problem:

The team had recommend a price for Tiffany & Co. to enter into negotiations for LVMH to acquire Tiffany. The recommendation is based on a comprehensive valuation analysis, including Comparable Companies, Precedent Transactions, Discounted Cash Flow (DCF), and Leveraged Buyout (LBO) analysis methodologies.

Research:

To begin the various analysis methodologies, we needed to produce Assumptions on Tiffany & Co.'s growth for the Base Case and Synergy Case. The Assumptions included projecting revenue growth projected for each main region for the next 3 years. Below is the Synergy Case Financials and Assumptions example:

The audience includes instructors and team members, seeking a thorough understanding of the valuation process and recommendations.

Process Details:

After we agreed on the assumptions, we had the tools to begin our independent evaluations. For the LBO, I first established the Sources & Uses of funds, then created a Debt Paydown Schedule for the transactions, and finally found a purchase prices that would given fit within the desired 20-25% range for the Internal Rate of Return. Those prices were $71.99 and $67.49, respectively.

Internal Rate of Return - 20%

Internal Rate of Return - 25%

Solution:

After creating a comprehensive valuation analysis, we recommended Tiffany & Co. enter into negotiations for LVMH to acquire Tiffany at a price of $133.00 per share.

Learnings:

  • The importance of thorough financial analysis to understand and defend the suggested purchase price of Tiffany & Co.
  • Assumptions will have a drastic impact on valuation outcomes. Garbage In, Garbage Out.
  • Team collaboration, attention to detail, and effective communication was crucial to producing the best recommendation and learning the most from the experience.
