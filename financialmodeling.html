Problem:

In the realm of high-stakes mergers and acquisitions, where every dollar counts, my Corporate Finance course group was tasked with creating a fair price for LVMH's potential acquisition of Tiffany & Co. The recommendated price is based on a comprehensive valuation analysis, including Comparable Companies, Precedent Transactions, Discounted Cash Flow (DCF), and Leveraged Buyout (LBO) analysis methodologies.

Research:

To begin the various analysis methodologies, we needed to produce Assumptions on Tiffany & Co.'s growth for the Base Case and Synergy Case. The Assumptions included projecting revenue growth projected for each main region for the next 3 years. Below is the Synergy Case Financials and Assumptions example:

The audience includes instructors and team members, seeking a thorough understanding of the valuation process and recommendations.

Learnings:

  • The importance of thorough financial analysis to understand and defend the suggested purchase price of Tiffany & Co.
  • Garbage In, Garbage Out. Assumptions have a drastic impact on valuation outcomes and need to be crafted carefully.
  • Team collaboration, attention to detail, and effective communication was crucial to producing the best recommendation and learning the most from the experience.

Role:

As the Leveraged Buyout (LBO) Lead Analyst, my analyzis focused on finding the purchase price per share of Tiffany that will allow LVMH to maximize their invesment will still being able to repay the debt taken on by the transaction.

My teammates George Bader, Willian Silva, Ekaterina Vanurina, and Justin Weiss completed the WACC Analysis, Comparable Companies, Precedent Transactions, Discounted Cash Flow (DCF). Collaborating together, we reviewed all of the evealution methods, explained out decisions, and taught to others what we learned throughout the process.
